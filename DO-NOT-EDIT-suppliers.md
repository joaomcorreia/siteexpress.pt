Yes, I checked. For SiteExpress, I would split print partners into 3 buckets: real reseller/white-label candidates, local Portuguese print partners, and backup online suppliers.

Best targets for partnership
1. Printmoz.pt — best white-label candidate

This is the most interesting one I found because they describe themselves as a white-label partner, saying customers will not see their name associated with the products. That is exactly the kind of setup SiteExpress needs if we later want customers ordering print products from a SiteExpress dashboard without exposing another supplier too much.

Use for: business cards, flyers, stickers, menus, simple print products.
Ask them: reseller prices, white-label delivery, neutral packaging, artwork rules, invoice flow, delivery times, complaints/returns.

2. PowerGraf — good local/reseller candidate

PowerGraf in Portimão openly mentions special values for designers, resellers and companies, and they offer business cards with several finishes and express production. That sounds like a company already used to working with intermediaries.

Use for: business cards, postcards, local Algarve-style print/product testing.
Why useful: small enough to talk, professional enough to produce, likely more flexible than huge online platforms.

3. Gráfica Sinal — explicit reseller pricing

Gráfica Sinal has “preços especiais para revendedores” visible on its business-card page, which makes it a strong candidate for a simple reseller agreement.

Use for: business cards first, then flyers/stickers if prices and delivery are decent.
Ask them: price table for resellers, minimum quantities, delivery to customer, whether they can ship without their branding.

4. NFCW.pt — digital/NFC business cards

NFCW is not normal print, but it is interesting because they offer digital/NFC business cards and mention special options for resellers or intermediaries. This could become a higher-margin add-on: “digital business card + QR + website link.”

Use for: smart business cards, QR/NFC cards, “modern contact card” packages.
Why useful: connects perfectly with SiteExpress because every card can point to the customer’s website.

Good local partner hunting source
APIGRAF directory

APIGRAF is the Portuguese association for the graphic, packaging and digital communication industries, and their directory is useful for finding real companies by area/product.

Two examples from APIGRAF:

IJF Print & Cut, Porto lists vehicle stickers, vinyl stickers, promotional gifts, vehicle decoration and flyers. That fits SiteExpress customers who need cards, van/vehicle branding, stickers and local advertising.

Tipografia Formosa, Porto lists posters, calendars and small-format/offset printing. This is more traditional print, but could be useful later for local Porto/north contacts.

Backup suppliers, not first-choice partners

These are useful if we need to fulfill print orders manually, but I would not treat them as proper partnership targets first:

360imprimir has cards, flyers, stickers and other marketing products, and is a big Portuguese online print player. Good for price checking and emergency fulfillment, but probably less flexible for a small SiteExpress partnership.

Pixartprinting offers a broad online catalogue: flyers, brochures, posters, catalogues, cards, labels, displays and fast delivery. Good supplier backup, not necessarily local-partner feeling.

Print24 has a large catalogue and even mentions affiliate/partner-shop options, including a web-to-print shop technology route. Interesting later, but probably too heavy for V1.

Vistaprint is useful for benchmarking business-card options and customer expectations, but it is more direct-to-consumer than partner/reseller.

My shortlist for SiteExpress V1

I would contact them in this order:

Printmoz.pt — because of white-label language.
PowerGraf — because they mention resellers/designers and seem approachable.
Gráfica Sinal — because they clearly mention reseller prices.
NFCW — because digital/NFC cards fit the website/QR idea.
APIGRAF local companies — for Porto/Lisbon/Algarve regional backup.
